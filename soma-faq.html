SOMA HEALTH SOLUTIONS INC. – FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

This FAQ outlines key information about Soma Health Solutions Inc., our AI-driven platform, and our commitments to ethics, privacy, and clinical utility. It is intended to support mental health professionals and organizations in understanding how Soma is used and how we operate.

GENERAL & REGULATORY

Are you medico-legally approved?

Soma is not classified as a medical device under any jurisdiction, including Health Canada or the FDA (U.S.). However, we actively engage with legal, clinical, and regulatory stakeholders to ensure our platform meets the highest standards of safety, transparency, and ethical oversight. Soma does not make clinical decisions; all outputs are drafts or suggestions intended to assist professionals, who retain full responsibility for client care. Consent templates and usage protocols reinforce this approach.

What stage is Soma currently at?

Soma is at Technology Readiness Level (TRL) 7, meaning the system is being demonstrated in an operational environment and is transitioning toward scalable deployment. It is currently in pilot with early partners and is transitioning toward limited commercial release.  Soma will be entering the market in a phased approach, first with clinician workflow focused tools, then with activity and program delivery to the client, then with advanced AI features.  Each of these phases will be offered as a pilot / pilot extension, upgrade then release structure.

Is Soma still in research, pilot, or ready for market?

Soma is in pilot and early deployment. While no longer in internal R&D, it is still undergoing active iteration with real-world feedback.  We are offering a pilot for our first iteration, which will be focused on clinician workflow.  We are considered market ready, we offer the pilot so that we can gather early feedback so our market launch will be as strong as possible.

PRIVACY & DATA

Do you sell data to third parties?

Absolutely not. Soma does not sell, trade, or monetize personal, health, or usage data. Third-party access is limited to service delivery (e.g., hosting, processing) and governed by strict confidentiality agreements.

Where is my data stored?

All data is stored securely in Canadian data centers, complying with PIPEDA and supporting regional privacy frameworks like HIPAA, and PHIPA as applicable.  

Where is the data physically stored?

Physically, data is stored in secure, high-compliance facilities located in Canada. No identifiable data is transferred outside Canada without lawful safeguards.

Who has access to the consultation information?

Only the licensed professional using Soma can access their consult data. Internal access is tightly restricted to authorized technical staff and only for maintenance or support, data use is controlled with opt-in user permission.  

What are the restrictions on client data used for training?

Client data is never used for model training without opt-in consent. We do not train on raw client sessions or journal content unless it is explicitly flagged by the user for opt-in research or feature improvement.  

What are the restrictions on professional data used for training?

Soma may analyze professional inputs such as template changes or editing styles in fully de-identified, aggregate form to improve usability and draft quality. No personally identifiable professional data is used for training.  We do not train on professional data content unless it is explicitly flagged by the user for opt-in research or feature improvement.  

Do you have a consent template for therapists to give to clients?

Yes. Soma provides a Client Consent Template that therapists can include in their processes. It covers client awareness and consent for recordings, journaling, and data privacy, emphasizing transparency and voluntary participation.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

What data have you used to train the AI?

Our AI is developed in collaboration with science-focused large language model providers (e.g., Anthropic), using a mix of publicly available and licensed datasets. Soma’s proprietary components are trained exclusively on anonymized user and system usage data. Importantly, user data is only used for training if the user has explicitly opted in.

As of now, Soma has not released any models trained on user data. We are committed to full transparency regarding our training sources, testing procedures, and release plans during any pre-release phase. For more details, please refer to our Terms of Pilot or Software-as-a-Service agreement.

Are there populations that might be excluded in this data?

Like most large AI systems, training data may underrepresent certain cultures, languages, or marginalized groups. Soma is actively building validation methods—including practitioner and client opt-in surveys—to identify and address bias or blind spots.

Am I able to train the AI for my own preferences?

You can’t fine-tune the model directly yet, but your edits, templates, and feedback influence how Soma adapts to your workflow. Personalized tools (e.g., note structure, terminology preferences) are currently in development.  Further, custom AI preferences are currently in research and development.

What is the extent of your predictive measures?

Soma does not predict diagnoses or outcomes. Its AI is designed to summarize sessions, assist with documentation, and support workflow.

Does it only use individual data to determine suggested practices, or does it use population-based data as well?

Currently, Soma uses generalized language models and does not generate personalized treatment recommendations. Future population-level analytics will be opt-in, anonymized, and used only to inform practitioner decisions.

Is there any diagnostic relevance to the output?

No. Soma does not suggest or infer diagnoses. All AI-generated content is designed to support, not replace, clinician judgment and must be reviewed and approved by the professional before use.

CLINICAL IMPACT & USABILITY

What does your data say about the clinical relevance for this app for clients (e.g., decrease in anxiety symptoms)?

Our early feedback shows promise in reducing clinician burnout, improving session focus, and streamlining documentation. Formal studies on clinical outcomes (e.g., symptom reduction, therapeutic alliance) are in progress. Soma is committed to independent validation through ethics-approved research and peer-reviewed publication.  We are currently in an HREA approved trial that is focused on data collection of anxiety signals from individuals.  Further research will be announced post approval with HREA.

What is the accuracy of the recommendations/client notes?

Soma is designed to save time by producing well-structured first drafts, not to replace professional judgment or editing.  Our accuracy will grow with your feedback, however, early results show that in most cases little editing is needed.  Please review all AI output for accuracy, all AI can make mistakes.

SYSTEM DESIGN & SAFETY

Does the AI make autonomous decisions?

No. Soma’s AI has no independent agency. It cannot make clinical decisions, initiate actions, or offer treatment recommendations. All content generated by the system is created only in response to practitioner input, such as note generation, summarization, or documentation prompts.

Can the AI provide diagnoses or treatment suggestions?

No. As per Soma’s Terms of Use, all AI-generated content is strictly a suggestion and must be interpreted as such by the licensed professional. Any phrasing that may appear clinical is generated as a draft to support documentation only, and is not to be taken as a diagnosis, treatment plan, or therapeutic direction. It is the responsibility of the professional to review, interpret, and revise all content before it is used in a clinical or client-facing context.

What safeguards are in place to prevent misuse?

Audit logs: System activity is logged to support transparency and accountability in usage.

What are the programming restrictions?

Soma’s system is built under strict ethical and legal boundaries:

  • It cannot store or process identifiable health information.
  • It cannot access or transmit data beyond approved secure environments.
  • It is prohibited from autonomous behavior, including initiating interactions or making decisions without direct user instruction.

Is the system designed to comply with regulatory standards?

Yes. Soma’s development aligns with:

  • Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA)
  • International frameworks (HIPAA, PHIPA)

The platform is built to evolve with ongoing changes in regulatory requirements, with legal oversight and regular internal audits to ensure compliance.
