(a)         Optional Connection – A connection between a client’s Soma mobile app account and a Provider’s Soma professional account is strictly optional. Either the client or the Provider may end the connection at any time through the Platform settings or by contacting Soma.

(b)         Client Control of Shared Data – The client determines which information is shared with the Provider through Soma. This may include:

  • Journal entries
  • Mood or stress ratings
  • Time, date, duration, and completion states of activities
  • Audio recordings or transcripts (if separately consented)
  • Wearable device data (if separately consented)

Soma will only transmit the specific categories of data that the client has explicitly chosen to share.

(c)         Anonymity and Encryption –
        (i)         All client data shared through Soma is anonymous to Soma, and where technically feasible, is pseudonymized using Provider-assigned aliases.
        (ii)         Sensitive client data, including journal entries, activity details, and wearable device credentials, is encrypted. Soma will not decrypt such data without the client’s explicit consent, and in the case of wearable credentials, Soma will not decrypt them under any circumstances, even if consent is provided for product improvement.

(d)         Audio Recording Rules – If a Provider records a client session for transcription purposes:
        (i) The Provider must obtain explicit consent from the client before recording begins. Such consent may be obtained in writing, electronically, or through Soma’s consent mechanism.
        (ii) Audio recordings may be used solely to create a transcript that may be summarized for clinical note drafts.
        (iii) Audio recordings are retained for no more than twenty-five (25) days from the date of recording.
        (iv) Audio recordings and transcripts will not be used for any other purpose without the client’s further explicit consent.

(e) Wearable Device Data Rules – If a Provider requests wearable device data:
        (i) The client’s connection to a wearable device is optional and may be refused even if the Provider has enabled wearable integration as an option.
        (ii) Any wearable data collected through Soma remains anonymous.
        (iii) The types of wearable data that may be collected include, without limitation, heart rate, oxygen levels, breathing rate, sleep data, temperature, and activity data.
        (iv) Soma will summarize and share wearable data with the Provider only if the client has explicitly consented to connect a wearable device.
        (v) If the client consents to anonymous wearable data collection, Soma may use such data to improve the product in addition to any other data the client has consented to share for improvement purposes.

(f) Provider Responsibility for Shared Data – Once any shared data (including journal entries, mood ratings, activity completion states, audio recordings, transcripts, or wearable data summaries) is received by a Provider, the Provider is solely responsible for:
